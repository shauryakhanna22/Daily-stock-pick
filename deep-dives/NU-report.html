Business Overview

Nu Holdings (Nubank) is the world's largest independent digital bank by customer count, founded in 2013 by David Vélez (ex-Sequoia Capital). The company disrupted Brazil's highly concentrated banking oligopoly by offering zero-fee credit cards, digital accounts, and consumer credit with no physical branches. The result: a cost-to-serve of just $0.80/month per active customer — 85% below Brazilian incumbents — and a customer acquisition cost of $6.42 vs. $50–100+ for traditional banks.

Top 5 Investment Risks
🔴 FX & EM Currency Risk HIGH — Ongoing
~85% of revenue earned in Brazilian reals (BRL), reported in USD. Q4 2024: BRL depreciation caused Nu to miss USD revenue estimates despite strong local-currency growth. Currency-neutral FY2024 growth was materially higher than reported. A sustained 20% BRL depreciation reduces USD-reported revenue by ~17% with partial natural hedge (costs also BRL-denominated) but USD profits compress. Brazil's fiscal deficit concerns continue to pressure BRL structurally. This is the single largest systematic risk for US investors in NU.
🟠 Credit Quality Deterioration MEDIUM-HIGH — Active Monitor
Q1 2026 provisions jumped 33% QoQ — above seasonal norms. Nu is deliberately expanding into higher-risk borrower segments (thin credit files, lower-income Brazilians). If Brazil unemployment rises or wages stagnate, NPL ratios could move materially higher, hitting revenues and requiring higher provisions simultaneously — a double negative on earnings. The Q2 2026 provision data will be critical.
🟠 Brazil Macro / Political Risk MEDIUM-HIGH
Brazil's Selic rate at ~13.75% creates funding cost pressure. Debt-to-GDP trending toward 100% raises fiscal sustainability concerns. Government intervention risk includes credit card rate caps, mandatory rate ceilings, or altered bankruptcy laws. The 2024 credit card installment regulation was a direct example of regulatory interference in Nu's core business.
🟡 Regulatory Risk — Banking Supervision MEDIUM
Brazil's BCB increasingly treats Nu as systemically important — higher capital requirements could reduce returns and growth. US bank charter application (capital-intensive, complex regulatory process). Interchange fee compression would directly hit credit card revenue.
🟡 Founder Concentration & Governance MEDIUM
David Vélez holds ~20% and has disproportionate voting control via multi-class shares — board cannot effectively challenge strategy. August 2025: Vélez sold 33M shares at ~$14 for "personal asset planning." While this is a small fraction of his total holding, a founder selling at near-current prices could signal his own assessment of fair value. No insider buying on the open market.

⚠️ WATCH — Lean BUY
Nu Holdings is arguably the best-executed large-scale digital bank outside of China. At 22x trailing P/E and ~16x forward P/E for 40–50% earnings growth, the stock is genuinely undervalued relative to growth. Analyst consensus is overwhelmingly bullish at $18–20 targets. The business model is structurally advantaged, founder-led, and has delivered consistently for 5+ years since IPO.

The single reason for WATCH over outright BUY is the Q1 2026 EPS miss driven by a 33% QoQ surge in credit provisions — above seasonal norms. Until Q2 2026 confirms this was seasonal and not a cycle turn, full conviction cannot be applied.

Recommended action: Establish a half-position now at ~$13 (within 14% of bear case downside, vs. 29–50% base/bull upside). Add to full position if Q2 2026 provisions normalise. Reduce if provisions remain elevated.
