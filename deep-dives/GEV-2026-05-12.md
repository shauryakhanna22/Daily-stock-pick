## 1. Business Overview

GE Vernova Inc. (NYSE: GEV) is one of the world's largest electric power equipment companies, spun off from General Electric in **April 2024**. The company designs, manufactures, and services technologies that generate, transfer, orchestrate, convert, and store electricity.

### Three Operating Segments:
1. **Power (~55% of revenue):** Gas turbines, steam turbines, generators, and lifecycle services. The crown jewel — approximately 7,000 installed gas turbines globally create an enormous, annuity-like services stream. Segment EBITDA margins are high and improving.
2. **Wind (~22% of revenue):** Onshore and offshore wind turbines. **The problem child.** Offshore wind has been losing money and the company has effectively exited new offshore orders. Onshore is recovering but still challenged by supply chain and pricing.
3. **Electrification (~23% of revenue):** Grid solutions, power conversion, solar & storage. The fastest-growing segment — up 32% revenue in Q4 2025 with EBITDA up 63% — driven by data center buildout and grid modernisation demand.

### Competitive Moat:
- Installed base of ~7,000 gas turbines creates a captive, long-duration service revenue stream (~70% of Power revenue from services)
- High switching costs in turbines (proprietary parts, trained technicians, OEM service contracts)
- Scale and track record in grid solutions — difficult for new entrants to displace in critical infrastructure
- Backlog of **$163 billion** as of Q1 2026 provides multi-year revenue visibility
- Geopolitical tailwind: "Energy security" theme has accelerated gas turbine demand in Europe and Asia

### One-Time Items & GAAP vs. Non-GAAP Flags

🚩 **Key one-time items to flag:**

1. **Offshore Wind Charges:** GE Vernova has taken multiple charges related to offshore wind:
   - ~$1.1B charge in 2023 for offshore wind losses (GE legacy)
   - Additional ~$700M charge in 2024 for offshore project cancellations
   - Q4 2025: Incremental accrual for **Vineyard Wind** delays (project halted by US government directive in December 2025; exact amount not disclosed but management called it an "incremental accrual")
   - Total offshore wind charges over 2022–2025: Estimated **$3–4B cumulative** — a significant drag on reported earnings

2. **Vineyard Wind Litigation:** Vineyard Wind is suing GE Vernova over allegedly defective blades, claiming 68 of 72 installed blades may be defective. A blade failure occurred in July 2024 causing a 6-month construction halt. This is an **ongoing contingent liability** — magnitude unclear but could be hundreds of millions.

3. **GAAP vs. Adjusted EBITDA:** Management cannot fully reconcile forward adj. EBITDA guidance with GAAP due to uncertainty around future restructuring charges. **Red flag for transparency**, though this is disclosed. Always verify adjusted EBITDA reconciliations when reported.

4. **Prolec GE Acquisition (2026):** GE Vernova completed full ownership of Prolec GE, effective from 2026. This adds ~$600–700M incremental revenue to 2026 — inflating comparisons slightly.

**Verdict on Earnings Quality:** Moderate. Underlying Power and Electrification earnings are high quality. However, offshore wind losses, litigation, and frequent one-time charges warrant scrutiny. GAAP-to-non-GAAP gaps are not unusual in capital-intensive industrials but investors should not rely solely on adjusted metrics.

US offshore wind has been additionally disrupted by a federal halt (December 2025). **Until this saga is fully resolved — either settled or written off completely — it will be a recurring drag on earnings and management attention.**

### Risk 2: Valuation / Expectations Risk ⚠️⚠️⚠️
At $1,075 and ~60x trailing P/E (30x forward on 2026 estimates), GEV is priced for perfection. Any guidance miss, any delay in the FCF ramp, any economic slowdown reducing data center buildout — and the stock could correct 25–40%. The stock is well above any reasonable DCF fair value (see Section 8).

### Risk 3: US Energy Policy / Regulatory Risk ⚠️⚠️
Power grid and energy transition investments depend heavily on US federal policy. The IRA (Inflation Reduction Act) tax credits are a risk if modified or repealed. Offshore wind has already been hit by the federal halt. GE Vernova's gas turbine business is actually benefiting from current US policy (favouring gas over wind) — but any reversal could impact the electrification segment.

### Risk 4: Execution Risk on Electrification Ramp ⚠️⚠️
The $2.4B of data center orders in Q1 2026 alone is extraordinary. This creates an execution challenge: can GE Vernova actually manufacture and deliver at this pace? Supply chain constraints, component shortages, and skilled labour availability could delay deliveries and push revenue recognition.

### Risk 5: Competition from Siemens Energy / Mitsubishi / MHPS ⚠️
Gas turbine competitors (Siemens Energy, Mitsubishi Heavy Industries Power Systems) are also benefiting from the same gas turbine demand surge. If competitors close the technology gap or offer more aggressive pricing, GEV's pricing power could erode. The company's advantage is the largest installed base globally — but competition is intensifying.
