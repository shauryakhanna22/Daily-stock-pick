1 · Business Overview

Boston Scientific is one of the world's largest medical device companies (~$20B revenue, 2025), developing minimally invasive devices across cardiovascular, endoscopy, urology and neuromodulation. The company's two segments — Cardiovascular (~60% revenue) and MedSurg (~40%) — span 10,000+ products sold globally.

The mid-February WATCHMAN inflection was presumably visible before the January investor day — the absence of a pre-call warning is concerning. Score: Medium confidence.

9 · Top 5 Risks
HIGH
PFA / EP Competitive Pressure
FARAPULSE had a monopoly window in 2024. Medtronic, J&J, and Abbott are now competing in the US PFA market. BSX expects US EP growth of only mid-single digits in 2026. Acceleration in share loss could impair the largest near-term growth driver.
HIGH
WATCHMAN Standalone Volume Decline
First-ever decline in standalone WATCHMAN volumes began mid-February 2026. Root cause not fully explained by management. If structural (reimbursement tightening, hospital budget pressure) rather than temporary, the $2B+ franchise faces multi-year pressure despite dominant market share.
MEDIUM-HIGH
Penumbra Acquisition Leverage
~$14.5B deal expected to close H2 2026 pushes net leverage to 3.5–4.5x EBITDA. Integration risk, management distraction, and limited financial flexibility for 3+ years of delevering. Penumbra's neurovascular devices are adjacent but not core to BSX's operational expertise.
MEDIUM
Tariff and Gross Margin Compression
Q1 2026 gross margin fell 100bps to 70.5%, partly from tariffs. Full-year 2026 gross margin guided "slightly below 2025." Ongoing US tariff policy creates multi-year uncertainty for a company with global manufacturing in Ireland, Costa Rica, and Malaysia.
MEDIUM
Regulatory / Product Safety Risk
PolarX cryoablation discontinued in Q1 2026 due to safety events. ACURATE discontinued in 2025. Device safety withdrawals carry revenue loss, litigation exposure, and reputational damage. With 10,000+ products, the probability of future withdrawals is non-trivial.

Not a BUY at $53.86

Boston Scientific is a world-class franchise experiencing a well-publicised reset. The 43% stock decline from ~$93 has brought valuation multiples to historically attractive levels. However, the DCF at any reasonable discount rate shows overvaluation, the guidance cut trajectory has no confirmed floor, earnings quality is impaired by structural GAAP-to-adjusted gaps, and the Penumbra acquisition adds leverage overhang.

Upgrade criteria: Q2 2026 WATCHMAN standalone stabilisation + EP share loss within guidance range + Penumbra financing disclosed conservatively. At $45–48, BSX would be a high-conviction BUY. At $53.86 today, it is a disciplined WATCH with a positive directional bias.
