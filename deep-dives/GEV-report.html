⚡ Business Overview 01

GE Vernova Inc. (NYSE: GEV) is one of the world's largest electric power equipment companies, spun off from General Electric in April 2024. The company designs, manufactures, and services technologies that generate, transfer, orchestrate, convert, and store electricity. With an installed base of approximately 7,000 gas turbines globally and a $163B backlog, GEV is at the center of the global energy transition.

Crown Jewel

Power (~55% Revenue)

Gas turbines, steam turbines, generators, and lifecycle services. ~7,000 installed turbines create a captive, annuity-like services revenue stream. ~70% of Power revenue comes from services.

Fastest Growing

Electrification (~23% Revenue)

Grid solutions, power conversion, solar & storage. Revenue up 32% in Q4 2025. Booking $2.4B in data center equipment orders in Q1 2026 alone — more than all of 2025.

Problem Child

Wind (~22% Revenue)

Onshore (recovering) and offshore (losses, litigation, exiting new orders). Offshore wind economics remain broken per management. Vineyard Wind blade failures and federal halt are ongoing issues.

The structural investment thesis is compelling: electricity demand is accelerating (AI data centers, EV charging, re-industrialisation), GEV is the dominant supplier of the gas turbines and grid equipment needed to meet that demand, and the services tail on 7,000 turbines creates a durable revenue floor regardless of near-term order volatility.

🚩 One-Time Items & Flags

FLAGOffshore Wind Cumulative Charges: ~$3–4B (2022–2025)

GE Vernova has repeatedly taken charges to write down offshore wind contracts: ~$1.1B (2023), ~$700M (2024 cancellations), plus incremental Vineyard Wind accruals in Q4 2025. These inflate reported losses and distort earnings quality. Until offshore wind is fully exited, expect more charges.

FLAGVineyard Wind Litigation — Defective Blades

Vineyard Wind is suing GE Vernova over allegedly defective blades (68 of 72 may be defective). A blade failure caused a 6-month construction halt. Settlement costs unknown but could be hundreds of millions. Active litigation = contingent liability on balance sheet.

NOTEGAAP vs. Adjusted EBITDA Reconciliation Gap

Management cannot provide GAAP reconciliations for forward adjusted EBITDA guidance "without unreasonable effort" due to restructuring uncertainty. While disclosed, this limits comparability. Always verify GAAP-to-non-GAAP bridges in quarterly filings.

NOTEProlec GE Acquisition (2026) Inflates Comparisons

Full consolidation of Prolec GE in 2026 adds ~$600–700M of incremental revenue. Year-over-year comparisons will flatter 2026 growth slightly. Organic growth vs. consolidated growth should be monitored.

⚠️ Top 5 Risks 08

HIGHOffshore Wind: Ongoing Losses & Vineyard Wind Litigation

~$3–4B in cumulative offshore wind charges 2022–2025. Vineyard Wind project halted by federal directive (Dec 2025); defective blades lawsuit ongoing; 68 of 72 blades may be defective. Until fully resolved, this is a recurring earnings drag and management distraction. Unknown but potentially large settlement liability.

HIGHValuation / Expectations Risk

At ~30x forward P/E and 41x P/FCF, any guidance miss or macro shock could trigger a 25–40% de-rating. The stock has tripled in a year; mean reversion is a real risk. Our bull case DCF only supports $1,000 — the stock is trading above that.

HIGHUS Energy Policy / IRA Risk

IRA tax credits are a key tailwind for electrification. Any repeal or curtailment reduces addressable market. The federal offshore wind halt demonstrates how quickly policy can change. Gas turbine demand is currently policy-tailwind, but a reversal is possible.

MEDIUMExecution Risk on Electrification Ramp

$2.4B of data center equipment orders in Q1 2026 alone is extraordinary growth. Delivering on this backlog requires supply chain scaling, component procurement, and skilled labour. Any manufacturing or delivery delay would push revenue recognition and disappoint street expectations.

MEDIUMCompetition from Siemens Energy / MHPS

Gas turbine competitors are also benefiting from the demand surge. If Siemens Energy or Mitsubishi close the technology or service gap, GEV's pricing power could erode. The advantage of 7,000 installed turbines is durable but not permanent if competitors build a competing installed base.
