## Market Context
AI infrastructure investment continues to accelerate in May 2026, with semiconductor, cloud, and energy-transition companies reporting record backlogs and consensus price-target upgrades; simultaneously, wide-moat consumer, pharmaceutical, and enterprise-software names remain deeply discounted relative to Morningstar's intrinsic-value estimates — in some cases by 38–57%. With the risk-free rate near 4.3%, stocks offering FCF yields above 5% represent compelling risk-adjusted alternatives across multiple sectors.

### #6 - MU: Micron Technology, Inc.
**Sector:** Technology / Semiconductors / AI Infrastructure | **Cap:** Mega Cap ($853B) | **Conviction:** HIGH

**Why undervalued now:** Micron trades at a PEG ratio of just 0.2x — one of the most extreme mismatches between earnings growth and stock price in the entire S&P 500. Full-year consensus EPS is projected to grow 611% YoY on surging HBM3E (High Bandwidth Memory) demand from AI GPU clusters. Even at $745, growth-adjusted valuation is extremely compelling. 27 of 30 covering analysts are bullish.

**Future potential:** AI data centre build-out requires exponentially growing HBM and NAND capacity. Micron is one of only three global HBM suppliers (alongside Samsung and SK Hynix), with the highest HBM3E yield among Western producers — a structural competitive advantage. Analyst price targets reach $1,100 on the high end.

**Key risk:** Memory is cyclical; DRAM/NAND oversupply could materialise in 2027 if AI capex slows. Sell-side consensus mean target ($657) may lag the stock's recent AI-driven re-rating, reflecting analyst caution rather than fundamental weakness.
