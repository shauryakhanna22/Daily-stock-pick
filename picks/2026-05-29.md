### #2 - CRNX: Crinetics Pharmaceuticals

Q1 2026 revenue surged to $10.7M (vs. $0.4M a year ago), with 70% of patients already receiving reimbursed therapy — an exceptionally strong payer acceptance rate for a newly launched rare-disease drug. **Key risk:** Binary clinical/regulatory risk on pipeline candidates; single-product concentration in near term; reimbursement access could narrow if payors tighten criteria for oral vs. injectable acromegaly drugs.

### #3 - SCHW: Charles Schwab Corporation
**Sector:** Financials / Wealth Management | **Cap:** Large Cap ($163B) | **Conviction:** HIGH

**Why undervalued now:** SCHW at ~$92 is well below its $118–127 analyst targets as the market has not yet priced in cash-sorting normalization — the process by which uninvested client cash migrates back from money market funds into Schwab's interest-bearing sweep accounts, directly boosting net interest income. FY2026 guidance came in ahead of consensus estimates, prompting Barclays to raise its target to $127 (Overweight) citing structural improvement in the NII outlook.

**Future potential:** SCHW has 35M active brokerage accounts and $9T+ in client assets — the integrated Ameritrade platform creates unmatched economies of scale in wealth management. As the rate environment stabilizes, NII recovery toward $15B+ annually will re-anchor the stock at 20x+ earnings. Raymond James has a $137 target.

**Key risk:** Interest rates declining faster than expected; competitive brokerage fee pressure from zero-commission rivals; any compliance or operational issues with the TD Ameritrade integration.
