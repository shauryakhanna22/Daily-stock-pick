## Market Context
US equity markets are navigating a resilient macro backdrop in late May 2026, with the Fed having completed 1–2 rate cuts from peak levels and the 10-year yield hovering near 4.3%; AI infrastructure spending remains the dominant secular growth catalyst, driving rotation toward semiconductors and energy infrastructure. Consumer discretionary and healthcare managed care offer deep value dislocations as tariff headwinds, China exposure concerns, and Medicaid reimbursement uncertainty push several wide-moat franchises well below their intrinsic values.

### #1 - MU: Micron Technology
**Sector:** Technology / AI Semiconductors | **Cap:** Mega Cap ($880B) | **Conviction:** HIGH

**Why undervalued now:** Micron trades at a forward P/E of ~12x and a PEG ratio of 0.2x — the most statistically undervalued large-cap semiconductor on the planet. The market has historically applied cyclical memory multiples to what is now a structurally elevated AI memory super-cycle driven by HBM3e demand for Nvidia's GB200/GB300 racks. On May 26, 2026, UBS tripled its price target to $1,625, citing a permanent re-rating of memory earnings durability; 43 of 46 covering analysts already carry Buy or Strong Buy.

**Future potential:** UBS projects Micron will generate over $400B in cumulative free cash flow from 2027–2029. AI inference infrastructure consumes 6–8x more HBM per rack vs. prior GPU generations, creating a durable demand floor. Fiscal 2026 revenues are expected to grow 111% YoY, followed by another 36% in FY2027.

**Key risk:** Memory cycles can reverse sharply if AI capex moderates; HBM supply ramp from Samsung and SK Hynix could compress margins earlier than expected.

### #9 - NKE: NIKE Inc.
**Sector:** Consumer / Athletic Footwear & Apparel | **Cap:** Large Cap ($67B) | **Conviction:** SPECULATIVE
