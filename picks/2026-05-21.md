### #2 - MU: Micron Technology
**Sector:** Technology / AI Semiconductors | **Cap:** Large Cap ($110B) | **Conviction:** HIGH

**Why undervalued now:** Micron trades at just ~12x forward earnings despite analysts projecting 111% revenue growth in fiscal 2026, powered by an AI-driven HBM3e memory supercycle. The market has historically assigned memory chips a commodity multiple; the AI differentiation narrative for high-bandwidth memory hasn't yet been fully priced in relative to peers.

**Future potential:** HBM3e content per GPU training cluster grows exponentially as models scale — Micron is the only US-headquartered HBM supplier and is ramping capacity aggressively alongside NVIDIA. **Key risk:** Memory is cyclical — any demand softening in AI servers or PC/smartphone end markets could trigger another inventory correction. Samsung and SK Hynix remain formidable competitors in HBM capacity race.

### #4 - MOH: Molina Healthcare
**Sector:** Healthcare / Managed Care | **Cap:** Mid Cap ($9.6B) | **Conviction:** MEDIUM

**Why undervalued now:** Molina has been caught in the managed-care sector selloff driven by elevated medical loss ratios (MLRs) post-COVID normalization and Medicaid redetermination headwinds. **Key risk:** Medicaid rate resets can compress margins quickly; additional regulatory pressure on risk adjustment or CMS reimbursement changes represent the primary risk to the 2026–2029 earnings recovery thesis.

### #7 - STLD: Steel Dynamics
**Sector:** Industrials / US Steel & Reshoring | **Cap:** Large Cap ($30B) | **Conviction:** MEDIUM

**Why undervalued now:** Steel Dynamics at $234.68 trades at a steep discount to its estimated intrinsic value of $371 based on discounted future cash flows, with earnings forecast to grow at 20.7% annually over the next three years. US steel reshoring tailwinds from tariff policy and the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act underpin multi-year demand. The stock has been dragged down by near-term macro uncertainty rather than deteriorating fundamentals.
