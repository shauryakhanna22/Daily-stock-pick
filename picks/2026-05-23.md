# Daily Stock Picks - 2026-05-23

## Market Context
US equity markets in May 2026 are navigating elevated but plateauing interest rates (~4.3% 10-year Treasury yield), with the Fed holding steady as AI-driven productivity gains underpin corporate earnings revisions higher. Tariff uncertainty creates selective headwinds for consumer-facing names, while surging AI infrastructure demand and a record defence backlog provide durable secular tailwinds for energy, software, and industrials — widening the gap between quality compounders and fairly-valued or overvalued peers.

**Why undervalued now:** GEV trades at a meaningful discount to its intrinsic value despite reporting Q1 2026 adjusted EPS of $1.98 on $9.34B in revenue — ahead of consensus. Grid electrification spending is compounding as sovereign governments accelerate net-zero commitments.

**Key risk:** Execution risk on large multi-year contracts; potential for raw-material cost inflation; geopolitical tension affecting international project delivery.

### #2 - MSFT: Microsoft Corporation
**Sector:** Technology / AI Infrastructure / Cloud | **Cap:** Mega Cap ($3,160B) | **Conviction:** HIGH

**Why undervalued now:** Morningstar's April 2026 wide-moat analysis flagged MSFT as trading ~38% below its intrinsic fair value estimate — one of the widest discounts among mega-cap tech. Despite Azure growing at ~30% YoY and the Copilot AI suite driving enterprise seat expansion, the stock has lagged NVDA's re-rating and trades at a discount to historical forward multiples. Near-100% growth in cloud backlog confirms the revenue pipeline is accelerating into FY27.

**Future potential:** The combination of Azure (infrastructure), Microsoft 365 Copilot (productivity AI), and GitHub Copilot (developer tooling) positions MSFT as the full-stack AI vendor for enterprises. Analysts see 41% upside as Copilot monetisation ramps from pilot to production across Fortune 500 clients. The wide economic moat — enterprise lock-in, switching costs, and ecosystem breadth — is arguably strengthening, not narrowing.

**Key risk:** AI investment cycle peaking before monetisation matures; antitrust scrutiny on OpenAI partnership; margin compression from cloud infrastructure capex.

### #3 - KVYO: Klaviyo, Inc.
**Sector:** Technology / Software / Fintech | **Cap:** Mid Cap ($5.3B) | **Conviction:** HIGH

### #6 - SCHW: The Charles Schwab Corporation
**Sector:** Financials / Wealth Management | **Cap:** Large Cap ($160B) | **Conviction:** HIGH

**Why undervalued now:** SCHW has a wide economic moat built on low-cost brokerage, a massive cash sweep balance, and strong brand loyalty — yet shares trade well below Morningstar's fair value estimate. The stock is still recovering from the 2023 deposit outflow scare (unrelated to Schwab's fundamentals) and has not fully re-rated. With 28% upside to the analyst consensus mean and a Strong Buy from 15 of 16 analysts, the discount to fair value is hard to justify.

The completed TD Ameritrade integration has yielded cost synergies ahead of schedule, and advisor-channel growth continues to compound. Earnings are projected to double by 2028 as the balance sheet repositioning concludes.

**Key risk:** Faster-than-expected rate cuts reducing NIM; potential industry disruption from zero-commission fintech platforms; regulatory risk on payment-for-order-flow model.

### #8 - NOC: Northrop Grumman Corporation
**Sector:** Industrials / Defense | **Cap:** Large Cap ($101B) | **Conviction:** MEDIUM

**Why undervalued now:** NOC has carved out a wide economic moat in strategic defence through proprietary long-cycle programs (B-21 Raider, GBSD intercontinental missile) that no other contractor can replicate, yet the stock has declined 6.4% since its last earnings report despite solid fundamentals. The P/E of 24.4x is below defence sector peers given its quality of revenue, and NOC sits near the bottom of its 52-week range, creating a re-entry opportunity.

**Future potential:** Northrop's exposure to early-stage military development programs — which carry the highest margins and longest duration — means revenue and earnings visibility extends well into the 2030s. NATO allies increasing defence spending to GDP targets is a tailwind independent of US domestic budget cycles. The B-21 program ramp and GBSD production will be significant EPS drivers in coming years.

**Key risk:** US defence budget sequestration; execution risk on fixed-price development contracts; supply chain constraints on aerospace-grade components.

### #9 - HRMY: Harmony Biosciences Holdings, Inc.
**Sector:** Healthcare / Biopharma | **Cap:** Small Cap ($1.65B) | **Conviction:** MEDIUM

The stock sits 51% below the analyst consensus mean target of $43, with the median analyst forecast at $44. The market appears to be applying a binary biotech discount to what is actually a de-risked commercial business with a proven, FDA-approved product (WAKIX for narcolepsy/cataplexy).

### #10 - CROX: Crocs, Inc.
**Sector:** Consumer / Footwear / E-commerce | **Cap:** Mid Cap ($4.94B) | **Conviction:** SPECULATIVE

**Why undervalued now:** Crocs trades below its Simply Wall St estimated intrinsic value and carries one of the lowest forward P/E multiples in consumer discretionary. The stock has been depressed by tariff uncertainty, consumer spending concerns, and overhang from the HEYDUDE brand underperformance. The core Crocs business, however, remains highly profitable with gross margins above 50% and strong international momentum in Asia.

**Future potential:** Crocs is a rare consumer brand with genuine pricing power, a demonstrated ability to sustain cultural relevance across generations, and expanding DTC channels that improve margin mix. International markets — particularly Asia — represent substantial whitespace at less than 40% brand awareness penetration. HEYDUDE restructuring, if executed, could remove the primary earnings drag and unlock a re-rating to fair value.

**Key risk:** Tariff exposure on Chinese manufacturing; HEYDUDE execution risk; consumer trade-down risk in a softening discretionary environment; high short interest signal meaningful institutional scepticism.
