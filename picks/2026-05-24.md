## Market Context
Equity markets in May 2026 are navigating a mixed macro backdrop: the Fed has held rates steady after two cuts in late 2025, keeping the risk-free rate near 4.3%, while AI capital expenditure remains robust and a resilient jobs market supports consumer spending. Pockets of deep value have emerged in wide-moat compounders sold off on near-term concerns — semiconductors, enterprise software, and consumer staples — alongside healthcare names with blockbuster pipelines underappreciated by the market.

## Top 10 Undervalued Stocks

### #1 - MU: Micron Technology
**Sector:** Technology / AI Infrastructure | **Cap:** Large Cap ($128B) | **Conviction:** HIGH

**Why undervalued now:** Micron trades at a forward P/E of ~12x and a PEG ratio of just 0.2x — one of the most extreme mismatches between earnings growth and stock price in the S&P 500. The market has anchored on cyclical memory-chip concerns while ignoring Micron's dominant HBM3E DRAM position supplying NVIDIA's Blackwell GPU platforms and the secular shift to AI data center memory with far higher margins than commodity DRAM.

**Future potential:** HBM revenue is on track to exceed $10B+ annually within two years, driving structural margin expansion. With NAND and DRAM prices recovering and HBM commanding 3–4× the ASP of standard DRAM, EPS is projected to compound at 40%+ over the next 24 months, yet the stock is priced as if it remains in a trough.

**Key risk:** Memory cycles can reverse quickly; any AI infrastructure spending slowdown or inventory correction at hyperscalers would compress near-term earnings sharply.

### #2 - ANET: Arista Networks
**Sector:** Technology / AI Infrastructure | **Cap:** Large Cap ($78B) | **Conviction:** HIGH

**Why undervalued now:** Arista is the backbone of AI cluster networking — its ultra-low-latency Ethernet switching is now competing head-on with InfiniBand for hyperscaler GPU interconnects. Despite 20%+ revenue growth and gross margins above 64%, the stock has pulled back ~20% from highs as investors rotate out of infrastructure plays. Its wide moat is built on deep customer switching costs and proprietary EOS software embedded into the world's largest cloud networks.

**Future potential:** AI networking spend is estimated to grow from $10B to $40B+ by 2028. Arista's win rate at Meta, Microsoft Azure, and Google Cloud positions it to capture disproportionate share. Operating leverage is kicking in — EBIT margins expanding toward 40%+.

**Key risk:** Cisco's aggressive pivot to AI networking and potential hyperscaler in-sourcing of custom switching ASICs could erode share over a 3–5 year horizon.

### #4 - HRMY: Harmony Biosciences
**Sector:** Healthcare / Biotech | **Cap:** Small Cap ($1.08B) | **Conviction:** MEDIUM

**Why undervalued now:** Harmony is a rare-disease CNS specialist whose flagship narcolepsy drug pitolisant (Wakix) holds FDA-approved monopoly status in the US — no generic competition for years. The stock has drifted with the broader small-cap biotech selloff despite the company consistently beating earnings expectations and initiating a buyback program. At a forward P/E of ~12x for a rare-disease franchise, the valuation is compelling.

**Future potential:** A pipeline expansion into idiopathic hypersomnia and Prader-Willi Syndrome could more than double the total addressable market. Management has raised annual guidance, and the ~42.5% upside to analyst mean target reflects the market catching up to intrinsic value as pipeline catalysts materialize.

**Key risk:** Single-product concentration risk; pitolisant label expansion rejections or competitive CNS entrants could materially impair the thesis.

### #5 - CORT: Corcept Therapeutics
**Sector:** Healthcare / Biotech | **Cap:** Mid Cap ($6.1B) | **Conviction:** HIGH

**Why undervalued now:** Corcept's stock price of ~$56 sits materially below the analyst DCF-derived fair value of ~$84, a ~49% gap. The company generates consistent FCF from mifepristone (Korlym) for Cushing's syndrome — a glucocorticoid modulation platform with virtually no competition. Revenue grew at 20%+ in 2025, yet the stock trades at a below-market FCF yield, with insider buying signaling management conviction.

**Future potential:** The pipeline candidate relacorilant has demonstrated superiority over Korlym in Phase 3 data, potentially expanding to a broader patient population and extending the moat. Management confidence is reinforced by active share buybacks. A successful relacorilant approval could re-rate the stock significantly.

**Key risk:** Generic mifepristone challenges are an ongoing legal overhang; an adverse ruling could dramatically reduce Korlym revenue before relacorilant achieves full uptake.

### #6 - SCHW: Charles Schwab
**Sector:** Financials | **Cap:** Large Cap ($138B) | **Conviction:** HIGH

**Why undervalued now:** Schwab is a wide-moat brokerage with a durable cost advantage — its scale allows it to offer near-zero-cost trading while monetizing the multi-trillion-dollar client asset base through net interest income and asset management fees. After a temporary NII headwind from the 2023 bank-run fears and a massive deposit migration to higher-yielding money markets, Schwab's balance sheet has normalized and NII is recovering sharply as rate-sensitive deposits re-price.

**Future potential:** As deposit betas normalize and the asset base grows through organic client wins and the Ameritrade integration synergies fully flowing through, Schwab is positioned for 15–20% EPS CAGR into 2028. Wide moat + operating leverage + cyclical NII recovery = multiple expansion opportunity.

**Key risk:** A sharp rate-cut cycle would compress NII again; any regulatory overhaul of payment-for-order-flow revenue is a structural risk to the zero-commission model.

### #7 - GEV: GE Vernova
**Sector:** Industrials / Energy Transition | **Cap:** Large Cap ($108B) | **Conviction:** HIGH

**Why undervalued now:** GE Vernova is the crown jewel of the energy transition — its gas turbines, wind platforms, and power grid equipment are essential for the AI data center buildout, which is driving extraordinary electricity demand. Post spin-off from GE, the market is still calibrating Vernova's true earnings power as an independent entity. Backlog is at record levels and pricing power is strong, yet the stock's PEG ratio remains below market average.

**Future potential:** AI hyperscaler demand for reliable power (especially gas peaker plants to back up intermittent renewables) is a multi-decade tailwind. Grid modernization — including Vernova's HVDC and substation equipment — is a $50B+ global opportunity. Management has guided for accelerating FCF as margins expand from the current ~8% toward 15%+ over five years.

**Key risk:** Offshore wind remains an overhang — Vernova inherited GE's troubled offshore wind contracts, and cost overruns on legacy projects could weigh on earnings before that business is fully wound down.

### #8 - AVAV: AeroVironment
**Sector:** Industrials / Defense | **Cap:** Mid Cap ($5.6B) | **Conviction:** MEDIUM

**Why undervalued now:** AeroVironment dominates the small unmanned aircraft systems (sUAS) market with its Switchblade loitering munitions and Puma reconnaissance drones — battle-proven in Ukraine and now central to the US Army's procurement roadmap. The stock trades at a discount to defense primes despite growing faster, and the Strong Buy consensus reflects analyst confidence that the DoD's multi-year small-drone procurement will drive revenue at a 25%+ CAGR.

**Future potential:** The US Army's sUAS program of record, NATO allies' drone ramp-up, and AVAV's JUMP 20 and Vapor platforms for special operations create a $3B+ multi-year backlog opportunity. New international defense agreements are accelerating export revenue.

**Key risk:** Defense budget sequestration risk or a shift in DoD procurement priorities away from small drones could slow contract awards; AVAV trades at a premium multiple that leaves little room for execution misses.

### #10 - GLBE: Global-E Online
**Sector:** Consumer / E-commerce | **Cap:** Mid Cap ($5.3B) | **Conviction:** MEDIUM

**Why undervalued now:** Global-E is the under-the-radar infrastructure layer powering international e-commerce for enterprise brands (Shopify, Gap, Steve Madden, etc.). The company recently turned profitable — Q1 2026 net income of $30.36M — and earnings are projected to grow 31.9% annually as more brands localize their global checkout flows. The stock trades significantly below its estimated intrinsic value based on discounted future cash flows, while the market is still pricing in a money-losing growth company.

**Future potential:** Cross-border e-commerce is a $2T+ market growing at 20%+ annually, and Global-E's localization engine (40+ currencies, 100+ payment methods, local compliance automation) is deeply embedded in customer workflows. **Key risk:** Customer concentration (heavy Shopify dependency) and competition from native cross-border tools at Stripe and Adyen could limit long-term pricing power.
