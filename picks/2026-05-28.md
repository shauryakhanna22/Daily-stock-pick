# Daily Stock Picks - 2026-05-28
**Analyst:** Claude (claude-sonnet-4-6) | **Framework:** Growth + Quality + Valuation

## Market Context
US equity markets are navigating a bifurcated landscape in late May 2026: AI infrastructure spending continues to accelerate with hyperscaler capex in FY2026 on pace to exceed $350B collectively, while consumer-facing sectors and legacy software face margin compression from sticky inflation and a gradually cooling labor market. The Federal Reserve has held rates at 4.25–4.50%, keeping the risk-free rate near ~4.3% and placing a premium on FCF-generative businesses trading below intrinsic value — precisely where today's 10 picks cluster.

### #5 - GILD: Gilead Sciences

### #7 - MU: Micron Technology
**Sector:** Technology / AI Memory (HBM3E) | **Cap:** Mega Cap ($1,000B) | **Conviction:** MEDIUM

**Key risk:** NAND oversupply risk from legacy storage; geopolitical risk with China operations; DRAM cycle mean reversion risk post-AI capex peak.

### #10 - LRCX: Lam Research
**Sector:** Technology / Semiconductor Equipment | **Cap:** Large Cap ($139B) | **Conviction:** MEDIUM

**Why undervalued now:** Lam Research has delivered one of the best 12-month returns in the S&P 500 (+300%+ off its $79.49 52-week low) yet at $320, trades near its all-time high and close to the analyst mean target of $315. The investment case rests on analyst target revisions still lagging reality: Morgan Stanley upgraded LRCX to Overweight on May 2026 with a $331 target, citing accelerating etch and deposition tool orders as HBM3E capacity and advanced packaging expansions require Lam's unique technology.

**Future potential:** Lam's etch market share is near-monopolistic in several advanced node process steps (5nm/3nm/2nm). As TSMC, Samsung, and Micron expand HBM/advanced logic capacity in 2026–2027, tool orders are set to reach multi-year highs. EV/EBITDA of ~15x is reasonable for a near-monopoly in a structurally growing market. High end analyst target is $385 (+20%).

**Key risk:** Export controls restricting China revenue (~12% of mix); fab spending cycle mean reversion; customer concentration in TSMC.
