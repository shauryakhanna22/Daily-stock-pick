# Daily Stock Picks - 2026-05-25
**Analyst:** Claude (claude-sonnet-4-6) | **Framework:** Growth + Quality + Valuation

## Market Context
US equities remain in a bifurcated environment as of late May 2026: AI infrastructure and energy-transition names continue to attract institutional capital while rate-sensitive consumer and fintech stocks trade well below prior peaks, creating a rich hunting ground for value. Morningstar's Wide Moat Index notes the broad market still offers pockets of 25–40% discounts to intrinsic value, particularly in large-cap software and beaten-down consumer discretionary — the backdrop is favorable for disciplined stock-pickers.

### #3 - PODD: Insulet Corporation

**Why undervalued now:** Insulet's OmniPod tubeless insulin delivery system has captured the premium Type-1 diabetes market but the much larger Type-2 opportunity (10x the addressable market) is just beginning. ### #4 - HRMY: Harmony Biosciences Holdings
**Sector:** Healthcare / Biotech | **Cap:** Small Cap ($0.90B) | **Conviction:** MEDIUM

**Why undervalued now:** Trading at $28 with a P/E of ~11x, HRMY is one of the cheapest specialty pharma names in the S&P universe. WAKIX (pitolisant) for narcolepsy and idiopathic hypersomnia is a first-in-class H3 receptor antagonist with no serious abuse potential — a unique profile in sleep-wake medicine. With EPS expected to grow ~42% and the stock sitting at 11x earnings, even modest multiple expansion to 18x would drive >60% upside.
**Key risk:** Single-product concentration; a label expansion failure or generic challenge would materially reset the story.

### #5 - MU: Micron Technology
**Sector:** Technology / AI Infrastructure | **Cap:** Mega Cap ($760B) | **Conviction:** HIGH

**Why undervalued now:** Micron is the primary beneficiary of the HBM3E (High Bandwidth Memory) AI demand supercycle. Analysts forecast fiscal 2026 revenue of $108.7B (up from $79.8B), yet the stock trades at a PEG of just 0.2x — the most extreme earnings-to-price mismatch in the S&P 500. DRAM pricing discipline and NAND recovery are additional tailwinds the market underweights.
**Future potential:** HBM is a structural shift: every GPU cluster requires stacked memory, and Micron is one of only three suppliers globally (alongside Samsung and SK Hynix). AI server and data center refresh cycles drive a durable multi-year volume and ASP tailwind. 44 analysts carry a Strong Buy consensus.
**Key risk:** Memory markets are cyclical; a macro slowdown or AI capex pause could reset inventory cycles sharply.

### #9 - LRCX: Lam Research Corporation
**Sector:** Technology / Semiconductor Equipment | **Cap:** Large Cap ($80B) | **Conviction:** MEDIUM

**Why undervalued now:** Lam Research's etch and deposition tools are the linchpin of advanced semiconductor fabrication for both logic (TSMC, Intel) and NAND/DRAM (Micron, Samsung). The stock's PEG remains below 1.0x, and the AI-driven equipment upcycle is in its early stages as customers invest in next-generation node transitions. Stifel and B. **Future potential:** Gate-all-around (GAA) transistor transitions and 3D DRAM stacking are Lam-intensive processes that will drive equipment intensity per wafer higher. CHIPS Act subsidies accelerating US fab construction (TSMC Arizona, Intel Ohio) create a multi-year domestic order wave independent of Asia spending.
**Key risk:** China export restriction escalations remain the single biggest swing factor; China historically represented ~30% of Lam's revenue.
