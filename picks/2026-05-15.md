## Market Context
US equity markets in May 2026 are bifurcating sharply: AI-infrastructure megacaps command premium multiples while quality compounders in Consumer, Financials, and Healthcare trade 20–57% below Morningstar intrinsic value estimates, creating a rare simultaneous opportunity across multiple sectors. The Fed's hold-then-cut posture through 2026 is compressing credit spreads and supporting bank NIM expansion, while the AI-driven electricity buildout is accelerating orders for energy infrastructure players and semiconductor memory makers operating at historically cheap forward P/E multiples.

### #9 - MU: Micron Technology, Inc.
**Sector:** Technology / Semiconductors / AI | **Cap:** Mega Cap ($836B) | **Conviction:** MEDIUM

**Why undervalued now:** Micron trades at a forward P/E of just 12×—a fraction of the 25–30× that leading semiconductor peers command—despite being the dominant supplier of high-bandwidth memory (HBM) chips that power every major AI accelerator. Fiscal 2026 revenue is forecast to surge 111% with EPS reaching ~$58, making MU the highest-growth mega-cap trading at the lowest P/E multiple in the semiconductor space. The stock has outpaced older analyst targets (mean consensus $608), but Mizuho recently revised to $740 and Deutsche Bank set a bull-case target of $1,000.

**Future potential:** HBM chip demand compounds as NVIDIA's H200/B200 GPU shipments accelerate; Micron is the only US-headquartered HBM supplier, giving it strategic importance and pricing power. As the AI training buildout transitions to AI inference at the edge, Micron's LPDDR5 mobile memory becomes a second tailwind. At 12× forward P/E, a re-rating to 20× earnings would imply 67% upside from current levels.

**Key risk:** Analyst price targets have not fully caught up to the stock's rapid appreciation; if AI infrastructure capex decelerates in H2 2026, the HBM demand cycle could correct sharply. Korea/Taiwan competitive dynamics in DRAM could also compress margins.
