# Daily Stock Picks - 2026-05-20
**Analyst:** Claude (claude-sonnet-4-6) | **Framework:** Growth + Quality + Valuation

## Market Context
The AI capital expenditure supercycle is reshaping demand across technology, energy infrastructure, and defense — while the Federal Reserve's gradual easing path is unlocking compressed valuations in rate-sensitive large-caps. Pockets of deep value persist in wide-moat consumer turnarounds, beaten-down enterprise software, and overlooked mid-cap defense and healthcare plays trading well below both analyst consensus targets and intrinsic value estimates.

## Top 10 Undervalued Stocks

### #1 - MSFT: Microsoft Corporation
**Sector:** Technology / Cloud / AI | **Cap:** Mega Cap ($3,180B) | **Conviction:** HIGH

**Why undervalued now:** Despite a market cap north of $3T, Microsoft traded as recently as April 2026 at a ~38% discount to Morningstar's fair value estimate — a rare margin of safety for a mega-cap compounder. Azure cloud revenue is growing >40% YoY on AI inference workloads, and Copilot embedding across Office 365 creates a structural ARPU expansion story touching 300M+ business users. At a forward P/E of ~27x on high-confidence 14-16% EPS CAGR, the growth-adjusted valuation is cheaper than it appears.

**Future potential:** GitHub Copilot, Dynamics 365 AI, and Azure AI Studio lock enterprises into the Microsoft ecosystem at the infrastructure layer. The OpenAI partnership provides unmatched first-mover access to frontier model capabilities. EPS consensus projects $16-18 by FY2027, implying meaningful P/E compression at current prices if targets are hit.

**Key risk:** Antitrust scrutiny of AI model partnerships; Azure growth deceleration if hyperscaler capex moderates; OpenAI equity overhang.

### #2 - ANET: Arista Networks
**Sector:** Technology / AI Infrastructure / Networking | **Cap:** Large Cap ($179B) | **Conviction:** HIGH

**Why undervalued now:** Arista is the leading vendor for high-speed ethernet switching in AI data centers — its 400G/800G platforms are deployed at Meta, Microsoft, and Google at scale, and no credible competitor has matched its software-defined networking stack. At $141.58 the stock sits ~21% below its 52-week high and 32.8% below analyst mean target. Its wide economic moat, rooted in intangible assets and customer switching costs, is durable across AI capex cycles.

**Future potential:** The AI networking market is estimated to grow from ~$10B to $50B+ by 2030 as inference clusters scale. Arista's nascent campus networking product line expands its TAM by ~$10B. Gross margins of ~65% self-fund heavy R&D without dilution. International data center expansion in EMEA and APAC provides additional runway.

**Key risk:** Customer concentration (~40% revenue in top 3 hyperscalers); Cisco competitive response; Nvidia InfiniBand adoption at scale could reduce Ethernet-first deployments.

### #3 - GEV: GE Vernova
**Sector:** Industrials / Energy Transition | **Cap:** Mega Cap (~$276B) | **Conviction:** HIGH

**Why undervalued now:** GE Vernova is the primary beneficiary of the AI power demand supercycle — utilities need $100B+ in new generation capacity annually to power data centers and EV infrastructure, and GEV supplies gas turbines, grid equipment, and wind assets. **Key risk:** Offshore wind project execution risk; potential IRA subsidy headwinds from policy shifts; supply chain bottlenecks for large gas turbine components; interest rate sensitivity of utility capital programs.

### #4 - TEAM: Atlassian Corporation
**Sector:** Technology / Enterprise Software | **Cap:** Large Cap ($23B) | **Conviction:** HIGH

**Why undervalued now:** At $86.62, TEAM trades 65.7% below the analyst mean target of $143.51 — one of the widest consensus-to-price gaps among large-cap software. ### #5 - AVAV: AeroVironment
**Sector:** Defense / Industrials / Autonomous Systems | **Cap:** Mid Cap ($7.9B) | **Conviction:** HIGH

**Why undervalued now:** AeroVironment is the leading US manufacturer of tactical drones and loitering munitions, and its $161.52 share price represents a 96% discount to the 17-analyst mean target of $316.88. Revenue grew 14.5% YoY in FY2025 to $820M, and drone warfare demand from Ukraine and Indo-Pacific military positioning has materially expanded addressable markets. ### #6 - CORT: Corcept Therapeutics
**Sector:** Healthcare / Specialty Pharma | **Cap:** Mid Cap ($4.0B) | **Conviction:** HIGH

**Why undervalued now:** Corcept trades at $37.60 vs. a DCF-based intrinsic value of ~$83.61 — a 59.6% discount supported by near-monopoly positioning in cortisol modulation for treatment-resistant Cushing's syndrome. Gross margins exceed 95% on the Korlym franchise, and the company carries zero debt with consistent FCF generation. The mifepristone compound class is yielding early-stage oncology results that the market has largely not priced in.

**Future potential:** Relacorilant (next-gen selective cortisol modulator, cleaner safety profile) has a near-term FDA PDUFA date that could significantly expand the treated Cushing's patient population from ~5,000 to 30,000+. Pipeline programs in breast cancer and prostate cancer using mifepristone combinations represent binary upside optionality with favorable risk/reward at current valuation.

**Key risk:** Generic mifepristone competition risk; single-indication dependency; binary FDA event risk for relacorilant; small analyst coverage base (6 analysts) limits price discovery.

### #7 - SCHW: The Charles Schwab Corporation
**Sector:** Financials / Brokerage / Wealth Management | **Cap:** Large Cap ($173B) | **Conviction:** MEDIUM

**Why undervalued now:** Charles Schwab's economic moat — built on a durable cost advantage in retail brokerage and $6T+ in client assets — is not fully reflected in the $92.68 share price. Net interest margin compressed significantly during the 2022-2025 rate cycle as customers shifted cash into higher-yielding sweep alternatives; as the Fed eases, that NIM headwind reverses into a sustained tailwind. Deutsche Bank targets $128, Raymond James targets $137, and TD Cowen has also raised its estimate.

**Key risk:** Brokerage fee compression from Robinhood and zero-commission platforms; credit risk in margin loan book if markets correct sharply; regulatory changes to payment for order flow; further Fed rate cuts compressing net interest margin below current expectations.

### #8 - NKE: NIKE, Inc.
**Sector:** Consumer Discretionary / Athletic Apparel | **Cap:** Large Cap ($63B) | **Conviction:** MEDIUM

**Why undervalued now:** Nike is trading at $42.42 — approximately 57% below Morningstar's fair value estimate — making it one of the deepest discounts among wide-moat S&P 500 companies. ### #9 - STLD: Steel Dynamics
**Sector:** Industrials / Steel / Materials | **Cap:** Large Cap ($30B) | **Conviction:** MEDIUM

**Why undervalued now:** Steel Dynamics trades at ~$220 vs. a DCF-based intrinsic value estimate of $371.19 — an implied 69% fundamental discount — while analyst earnings forecasts call for 20.7% annual EPS growth significantly outpacing the broader US market. Q1 2026 net income of $403M on $5.2B revenue demonstrated resilient margins despite steel price cycles. Tariff-driven onshoring of US manufacturing and continued IIJA infrastructure spending underpin a structurally stronger demand base than the market is pricing.

**Future potential:** The new Sinton, Texas flat-roll steel mill is ramping toward full capacity, expanding STLD's volume base by ~30% from its pre-Sinton footprint. Steel demand from renewable energy installations (wind towers, solar racking structures), EV manufacturing facilities, and defense production provides secular demand tailwinds into 2028+. STLD's mini-mill model gives it a cost advantage over integrated blast-furnace producers.

**Key risk:** Steel price cyclicality; Chinese steel overcapacity pressuring global prices if tariffs are renegotiated; commercial and residential construction activity slowdown; scrap metal price volatility affecting input costs.
